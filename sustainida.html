A Call Towards Sustainibility

  • Shared Vision
August 14, 2020

Amidst this pandemic, we all have read many articles stating the carbon emission has lowered by 17 percent, seen photos of clear waters in Venice canal, clear Delhi skies. Many animals returned to their indigenous habitats where humans took over. But recently National Geographic has published an article where it has stated that these changes will be temporary but rather after this pandemic is over we will see an increased amount of pollution levels. And this might be true, as a recent study shows that the carbon emissions are only 5 percent lower than at the same point in 2019, even though normal activity has not yet fully restarted. Why is this so? “We still have the same cars, the same roads, the same industries, same houses,” says Corinne Le Quéré, professor of climate change at the University of East Anglia in Britain and lead author of the original study and subsequent update. “So as soon as the restrictions are released, we go right back to where we were.” All the healing done by the environment during this pandemic is temporary, and if we have to make it permanent we will have to make changes in our lives because change starts from us.


To make sustainable changes in our lives we will have to think right from the stuff we buy and use in our daily lives. But how do we know if it is sustainable and made from natural organic products which will not harm the environment? Sustainida, which is a portmanteau for Sustain and Nida (Nida is derived from an Arabic word which means ‘To Call’) so Sustainida means “A call towards sustainability”, is a startup trying to make an impact in the environment space. They have launched a platform to equip the customers by providing alternate Eco-friendly choices for commonly used products. What Sustainida does is, it hosts certified sustainable products from different vendors on its e-commerce platform, it has a wide range of products from eco-friendly pen to eco-friendly refrigerator, and everything is biodegradable and made from eco-friendly materials, and by following eco-friendly processes. Sustainida’s only mission is to bring about a paradigm shift of the society towards a sustainable way of living, ensuring all three P’s of sustainability, namely, Planet, People, and Partnerships.
